# Estimation Assumptions — 02_estimate.py
# Guanajuato State Carbon Tax × Federal IEPS × Mexico Pilot ETS

## Overview

Estimation tier: **Tier 3** (Pareto/size-based ETS coverage; fuel-level F fractions from inventory)

Three scenarios are computed: central, low, high. All values in GgCO₂e.

---

## 1. S scope (Guanajuato state carbon tax)

**Definition:** Direct emissions of CO₂, CH₄, and N₂O from stationary sources in the
industrial and energy sectors.

**In scope:**
- Industrias de la energía (energy industries) — stationary combustion + fugitives
- Industrias manufactureras y de construcción — stationary combustion
- Ladrilleras (brick kilns) — classified as stationary industrial in local inventory
- IPPU: Caprolactama (process N₂O)
- IPPU: Ácido nítrico (process N₂O)

**Out of scope:**
- Autotransporte (mobile) — 7,068.4 GgCO₂e
- Aviación (mobile) — 88.6 GgCO₂e
- Combustión agrícola (mobile) — 257.8 GgCO₂e
- Maquinaria de construcción (mobile) — 52.8 GgCO₂e
- Combustión comercial (not industrial) — 176.8 GgCO₂e
- Combustión residencial — 736.6 GgCO₂e
- HFC (not CO₂/CH₄/N₂O) — 306.8 GgCO₂e
- AFOLU — 2,521.1 GgCO₂e
- Desechos (Waste) — 1,433.8 GgCO₂e

**S total (central) = 6,622.1 GgCO₂e (34.4% of state total)**

## 2. F scope (Federal IEPS carbon tax)

**Definition:** Upstream fuel levy on all fossil fuels EXCEPT natural gas (Art. 2o-C).

### 2.1 Natural gas exemption

The NG exemption is the single most important structural parameter for Guanajuato.
Energy industries use 53.15 PJ NG vs 14.19 PJ non-NG (79%/21% by energy, 73%/27% by CO₂ mass).
This creates a large S∩E-only block: NG combustion in the refinery and large manufacturers
is covered by S and E but NOT by F.

### 2.2 Non-NG fuel fractions (F-covered within S scope)

Derived from IGCEI 2013 uncertainty table (Table 16, pp.68-70):

| Subsector | F-fraction (central) | Source fuels |
|-----------|---------------------|--------------|
| Industrias_energia | 0.267 | (combustóleo 1,079.6 + diesel 30.1) / 4,153.4 Gg CO₂ |
| Industrias_manufactura | 0.477 | (combustóleo + diesel + LP + aceites) / all fossil CO₂ |
| Ladrilleras | 0.50 | Assumption: 50% fossil, 50% biomass (range 0.30–0.70) |
| Caprolactama | 0.00 | Process N₂O, not fuel combustion |
| Acido_nitrico | 0.00 | Process N₂O, not fuel combustion |
| Emisiones_fugitivas | 0.00 | Fugitive CH₄, not a fuel sale |

**Note on gap in energy industries:** The uncertainty table lists CO₂ from three fuel types
totalling 4,153 Gg, while the results table shows 4,424 Gg (gap = 271 Gg, 6.1%).
The fuel fractions (73.3% NG, 26.7% non-NG) are applied to the results-table total,
so the gap is distributed proportionally. This is conservative (no fuel with unknown
F-exempt status is assumed to be non-NG).

### 2.3 Process emissions not covered by F

Process N₂O from caprolactama and ácido nítrico production is not generated by combustion
and is therefore not subject to the upstream fuel levy. These emissions fall in the
S∩E-only segment (covered by state tax and ETS, but not by federal fuel tax).

## 3. E scope (Mexico Pilot ETS)

**Definition:** Legal scope covers facilities ≥ 25,000 tCO₂e/yr. Non-binding pilot phase.
Treated as upper bound on coverage.

### 3.1 Independence assumption

ETS coverage fractions are assumed **independent** of fuel type fractions within each subsector.
This means the fuel mix of E-covered facilities is assumed equal to the sector average.

**Basis for Guanajuato:**
- The Salamanca refinery uses both NG (dominant) and fuel oil; its fuel split approximates
  the sector average for energy industries → independence plausible
- Large manufacturers in Bajío use LP gas and NG (both significant); sector-average
  fuel mix applies

**Sensitivity:** If NG-using facilities are systematically larger (more likely E-covered),
the S∩E-only segment would be higher and S∩F∩E lower than central. This potential
bias is partially captured in the low/high range for ETS coverage fractions.

### 3.2 ETS coverage fraction assumptions

#### Industrias_energia: central 95%, low 85%, high 99%

**Rationale:** The Salamanca refinery (PEMEX) processed 194,500 barrels/day of crude
in 2013 (IGCEI Tabla 4). Its combustion-related emissions are the vast majority of the
4,431 GgCO₂e attributed to energy industries — orders of magnitude above the 25,000 tCO₂e
threshold. Any co-located auxiliary operations (small support plants) add slight uncertainty,
hence 95% central rather than 100%.

Low 85%: allows for several smaller distributed operations below threshold.
High 99%: upper bound assuming essentially all energy industry emissions are from the refinery.

#### Industrias_manufactura: central 65%, low 50%, high 80%

**Rationale:** Guanajuato's Bajío automotive cluster includes:
- GM Silao: large assembly plant, definitely > threshold
- Mazda Salamanca: large assembly plant, definitely > threshold
- Honda and other major assemblers in the region
- Tier 1/2 suppliers: variable — some large enough to exceed threshold

Large food and beverage processors (e.g. grain processors, breweries), chemical
manufacturers also likely exceed threshold.

Standard Pareto estimate for a highly industrialised Mexican state. Sensitivity range
reflects uncertainty about the contribution of small-medium supplier factories.

#### Ladrilleras: central 0%, low 0%, high 0%

**Rationale:** Brick kilns in Guanajuato are predominantly family-scale operations.
No individual kiln expected to exceed 25,000 tCO₂e/yr. No range uncertainty.

#### Caprolactama: central 100%, low 90%, high 100%

**Rationale:** The caprolactama plant (Lanxess Guanajuato) produces 85,000 tonnes/year.
Process N₂O alone equals 202.7 GgCO₂e — more than 8,000× the ETS threshold.
Definitively E-covered. Low 90% adds marginal uncertainty about whether this specific
legal entity was included in the pilot ETS participant list.

#### Acido_nitrico: central 90%, low 70%, high 100%

**Rationale:** Production of 2,399 tonnes/yr generates 5.7 GgCO₂e from process N₂O alone.
As a standalone process, this is well above threshold. However, if this is a very small
standalone plant (not co-located with caprolactama), there is uncertainty about whether
it was individually registered in the pilot. Low 70% reflects this.

#### Emisiones_fugitivas: central 95%, low 85%, high 99%

**Rationale:** Fugitive CH₄ (4.8 GgCO₂e) comes from petroleum refining at Salamanca.
The refinery facility is E-covered; its fugitive emissions are attributed to the same
E-covered entity. Fraction mirrors the Industrias_energia fraction since they refer to
the same facility.

## 4. Ladrilleras fossil fuel fraction

**Central: 50%, Low: 30%, High: 70%**

Brick kilns in Mexico use a mix of biomass (firewood, sawdust) and fossil fuels (diesel, LP gas).
No Guanajuato-specific fuel survey is available. The 50% central estimate is based on
regional literature on Mexican brick kiln fuel use (INECC studies, USAID estimates).
This is the only parameter without a directly data-derived estimate.

**Impact on results:** Ladrilleras total is 12.1 GgCO₂e — minor relative to total S scope
(6,622 GgCO₂e). This assumption adds < 0.1% uncertainty to total coverage estimates.

## 5. Key structural comparison with other states

| State | Dominant overlap driver | Process CO₂? | NG role in S scope |
|-------|------------------------|--------------|-------------------|
| Durango (2022) | NG-dominated electricity → S∩E dominant | No | NG exempt from F → ~100% S∩E |
| Colima (2015) | NG-dominated electricity → large S∩E | No | As Durango |
| Morelos (2014) | Cement calcination CO₂ → S∩E-only process | Yes (cement) | Moderate |
| **Guanajuato (2013)** | **Refinery NG + process N₂O → large S∩E** | **No** | **High NG in refinery** |

Guanajuato is characterised by:
- The NG-heavy Salamanca refinery creating a very large S∩E-only block (~3,085 GgCO₂e)
- Caprolactama process N₂O adding to S∩E-only (~203 GgCO₂e)
- Manufacturing having a more balanced NG/non-NG split → meaningful S∩F∩E in that sector
- No process CO₂ from minerals (no cement, no glass, no metals smelting)
